Who It's For

Built for the people who need
assets to be visible, controllable,
and financeable.

The gateway removes integration barriers so every participant in the DER ecosystem can do their job.

Aggregators / VPP Operators

Need: more controllable MWs

Connect fragmented batteries, EV chargers, solar inverters, and flexible loads into one dispatchable fleet — without custom integrations for every OEM. Scale faster, earn more.

BESS Financiers

Need: real-time asset visibility

Monitor asset health, utilization, state of charge, degradation, and revenue potential across distributed storage portfolios. Prove performance. Reduce risk.

OEMs

Need: standards compliance

Make hardware interoperable, telemetry-ready, and dispatch-ready without building a full DERMS or aggregator platform internally. Sell into more markets, faster.

Utilities / DISCOMs

Need: grid visibility and control

Gain visibility into behind-the-meter assets and coordinate flexible capacity for peak reduction, grid support, and demand response programs through open standards.

Energy AI Platforms

Need: clean tools and context

Use standardized telemetry, MCP context, and dispatch APIs to build AI agents, optimization engines, and market participation tools — on top of real, trustworthy data.

Commercial Asset Owners

Need: savings and monetization

Turn solar, batteries, EV charging, and controllable loads into operational assets that reduce costs and participate in grid programs without managing complexity.

Moat

Hardware + software creates the cost advantage.

The gateway creates real access to physical devices. The software turns that access into a scalable control layer. Together they reduce integration costs and create a defensible data and dispatch network.

Hardware wedge Local access, reliable telemetry, offline resilience, and secure device control without OEM dependency.
Software scale Common asset models, dispatch APIs, verification, analytics, and MCP tools compound across every device connected.
Network effects More connected devices attract more aggregators, AI apps, and financiers — which in turn creates more demand from OEMs to be compatible.
